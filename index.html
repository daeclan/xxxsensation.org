XXXTENTACION (Jahseh Onfroy, 1998-2018) was arguably the most polarizing musical artist of his generation. The music he made was creatively diverse and undoubtable. Yet the negativity he entered the game with was also undoubtable. Videos of violent assaults, allegations of aggravated battery of a pregnant woman, false imprisonment, and attempted murder surrounded the young rapper as he rose in the ranks, ultimately becoming recognized as the unoffical king of the "Soundcloud rap" era and an internationally recognized hero to many young fans.

The XXXSENSATION project began in February 17, 2017 and ran through March 29, 2018. Jahseh Onfroy was shot and killed during a robbery on June 18, 2018. He was 20 years old.

This website is the untold story of this project, as told by me, Declan (fka XXXSENSATION), about how his music changed my life and how my (remaking his) music changed his, and how these dueling internet phenomenons changed the future of the rap game.

ORIGINS: DRAKE

PART ONE: OCTOBER 2016 - APRIL 2017


To tell the story of XXXSENSATION, one must first be aware of the events that led to its creation. This story will be told through three distinct eras and three titans of modern rap: Drake, Kendrick Lamar, and Kanye West.

In mid-2016 Aubrey "Drake" Graham was in his early 30's and had recently released his album Views, a poorly received album that a majority of reviewers "found to be an overlong retread of Drake's previous work." By this time in his career, Drake was notorious for biting flows of up and coming rappers to freshen up his own sound, squeezing the juice out of the flows before discarding them‚Äîa move a lot of people criticized for being creatively unoriginal & artistically unfair. To make matters worse, Drake had recently been accused of employing a "ghost writer," which is when one rapper hires another rapper to write their lyrics and flows. Having a ghost writer is sacrilegious in the rap game, as one's artistry and status are defined by one's writing ability (pen) and delivery (flow). That accusation took Drake out of a lot of people's Top 10 discussion, as it was akin to finding out Michael Jordan had hired a body double and was watching his games at home with a cold one.

With these predatory techniques, Drake escaped accusations of being a washed-up child-actor and evolved himself into a bonafide rapper-singer who dominated the pop and rap charts for years. Drake had signed to Lil Wayne's Young Money Records in 2009 and quickly established dominance in the newly opened "melodic rap" lane that Kanye had opened when he released 808s & Heartbreak (2008). Drake took full advantage of this new subgenre, releasing Take Care (2011), NWTS (2013), IYRTITL (2015) and his collaboration with Future, What A Time To Be Alive (2015). This is to say, he had come off an undeniably hot run of releases and was blindsided by the lukewarm reception of his Views (2016) project. While we can't definitively confirm that Drake was actively hunting for new flows to bite in mid-2016, it would fit the pattern that his team would be scanning the horizon for the next big thing.

Meanwhile, 1500 miles south of Drake's native Toronto, Jahseh Onfroy, known to his fans as XXXTENTACION, found himself sitting in Metro West Detention Center outside of Miami, Florida facing serious legal troubles. He had been arrested and charged with "beating his pregnant girlfriend, strangling her, forcibly confining her against her will, and then trying to bribe her not to testify against him." The harrowing descriptions that his victim gave in her deposition are even more disturbing than his charges suggest. A track he had released ten months earlier in December 2015, "Look At Me," had blown up, gaining millions (and millions) of plays in response to the media furor surrounding the controversial subject matter of what he had been accused of. While in jail, more and more blogs and news outlets were sharing his story, some delighting in the villainy and antihero nature of this young kid from Florida. XXXTENTACION's notoriety and web presence grew exponentially, accelerated by the morbid curiosity surrounding his allegations.

In early 2017 as XXXTENTACION sat in jail waiting for a chance at bail, Drake debuted a snippet of a new song entitled "KMT" (short for Kissing My Teeth) at the Amsterdam stop of his Boy Meets World tour. A concert video was uploaded the next day, and would forever change the course of rap history. While the song's beat and production was unique, the flow and delivery Drake used was undeniably bitten from XXXTENTACION's hit "Look At Me." It was a 1:1 copy of X's flow, and was in no way an accident given Drake's proclivity to steal and repurpose other rapper's styles. Mashups of the two songs were made‚Äîfurther proving the case‚Äîand heated accusations were hurled at Drake's camp from the groundswell of young XXXTENTACION fans.



From listening to his work, it was clear the young rapper had musical talent and wild energy, but the darkness in his story and lyrics painted a harrowing picture of who he may be underneath it all. It felt like a young Anakin Skywalker entering the game‚Äîthe limit of his potential was immeasurable, but it carried with it the malevolence and negativity that turned that young Anakin into Darth Vader.

As an extremely online millennial, XXXTENTACION was unavoidable. I had heard "Look At Me" a few times and was feeling guilty for even listening, let alone enjoying the music of someone who had been accused of and jailed for such heinous violence against women. However, this young rapper was clearly gifted. The sample and production were insane, especially played very loudly. And the real truth is, X was publically hating on Drake, which was exciting and fun (at times) to see. A young new energy challenging the old guard.

I thought it‚Äôd be funny to intentionally steal X‚Äôs flow from "Look At Me" (the very same flow he accused Drake of stealing) and make a positive parody of the violent viral hit while satirizing Drake's accusations and fading role in the rap game. The lyrics of XXXTENTACION's "Look At Me" threatened aggressive sexual and physical assaults and were screamed at the top of his lungs. To demonstrate the absurdity of his subject matter, I rewrote the lyrics to be gentle, positive, and kind to women, but rapped it in the exact same cadence and also screamed my little lungs out, too. The goal of the first track was to make people laugh, but the project quickly got more serious, becoming an outlet to directly criticize the misogynistic Instagram Live sessions he'd have, or the violent and brutal nature of XXXTENTACION's marketing campaigns. As the project grew in scope, many fans were divided‚Äîwas XXXSENSATION a fan project, or was it making fun of XXXTENTACION? Where did the joke end and the commentary begin? With that confusion arising, the lyrics and songs I rewrote got progressively more targeted and critical of his behavior.

While I respected the lyrical dexterity and clear talent that X had, the XXXSENSATION project created in protest of his ascension, not despite but because of his violent persona. The project worked as a satirical examination of his bad-boy character through the recomposition of his works, removing the negativity and darkness and replacing them with positivity and brightness. It was clear XXXTENTACION was rapidly ascending the public consciousness by the people speaking out about his music (namely Kanye and Kendrick), but my project was more about the exposing and playing with the ‚Äúkayfabe‚Äù of rap; the artistic choices that rappers make to define and promote a persona.

A MIRROR

"...a wave reflector. Light consists of waves, and when light waves reflect from the flat surface of a mirror, those waves retain the same degree of curvature and vergence, in an equal yet opposite direction, as the original waves. "




The context of the culture at this particular time is important to understanding the nature of this project. Trump had just been inaugurated as the 45th president of the United States of America. It felt like America itself had become a parody, or a dismal episode of a Reality TV show. Rap music was in a strange place, with the racial unease and general darkness looming a lot of heads turned to rappers preaching positivity as a different approach to the violent, gang-affiliated trap music that was coming out of Atlanta (e.g., Future, Migos, Young Thug).

XXXSENSATION recognized this yearning for something uplifting, but it was important that it still sounded dope. There were rappers who were trying to be that positive voice, but they ended up sounding unintentionally corny (looking at you Kyle & Lil Yachty). The only rapper who seemed to do this authentically was Lil B, but the world wasn't ready for that yet. Instead, the zeitgeist was focused on trap music‚Äîspecifically triplet flows about whipping up dope coming out of Atlanta.

In real life (irl) there was Jahseh Onfroy, a black teenager from Plantation, FL and there was me, Declan Murphy, a white dude in his mid-20‚Äôs from Bar Harbor, ME. Two men from the East Coast of America, one from pretty much as South as you can get and one from pretty much as North as you can get. Each about 100 miles from their respective border, whether that be Canada or the Florida Keys.

On the internet, though, we could be heroes or villains. XXXTENTACION & XXXSENSATION. Yin & yang. This spoke to our backgrounds. Where we were born, what our homelives were like. Our nature & our nurture.

HELPING HAND


XXXTENTACION (XT) came into the game with #BADVIBESFOREVER as his mantra. His whole aesthetic was negativity, violence, and misogny. As a counterpoint, XXXSENSATION's (XS) position was #GOODVIBESFOREVER and his songs, tweets, and merch reflected this positivity and contrast.


As XT's star grew, he began to shift his energy away from negativity and, in doing so, started copying the very actions that XS had done months prior. Most notably, in May 2017 XS began a "hashtag challenge"‚Äî#OneMillionForOurWomen‚Äîto raise $1,000,000 for women's rights and victims of domestic abuse.
