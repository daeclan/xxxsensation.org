Hi there! My name is Declan, and in February 2017 I created a parody of a rapper, XXXTENTACION, and called the project XXXSENSATION.

For those unacquainted, XXXTENTACION (Jahseh Onfroy, 1998-2018) was arguably the most polarizing artist of his generation. The music he made was creatively diverse and undoubtable. Yet the negativity he entered the game with, born from a rough home life and upbringing, was also undoubtable. Allegations of sexual assault, domestic kidnapping, and attempted murder surrounded the young rapper as he rose in the ranks, being donned the unofficial king of "Soundcloud Rap," and ultimately becoming an internationally recognized hero to many young fans.

The XXXSENSATION project began in February 17, 2017 and ran through March 29, 2018. Jahseh Onfroy was shot and killed during a robbery on June 18, 2018. He was 20 years old.



During these 13 months, the project was both applauded by XXXTENTACION, then blocked and then unblocked by XXXTENTACION. As these interactions happened, I watched as XXXTENTACION's music, outlook, and behavior began to change in a positive way. He began emulating direct aspects of the XXXSENSATION persona, and had he not been tragically taken from us, I believe he would have continued that transformation‚Äîfully negating the need for XXXSENSATION to exist as a parody.

The XXXSENSATION project and persona rose from recognizing the potential in XXXTENTACION's mind and music, while criticizing the violence and misogny his brand(#BadVibesForever) respresented. My goal was to provide a counterpoint to that darkness, transforming his songs from negative, violent confrontations to positive, loving anthems. This website is the untold story of this project, as told by me, Declan (fka XXXSENSATION), about how his music changed my life and how my (remaking his) music changed his.

The goal of the first parody was to make people laugh, but the project quickly got more serious, becoming an outlet to directly criticize the type of violent, misogynistic darkness and violence that X was leading with. As the project grew in scope, many fans were divided‚Äîwas XXXSENSATION a fan project, or was it making fun of XXXTENTACION? Where did the joke end and the commentary begin? With that confusion arising, the lyrics and songs I rewrote got progressively more targeted and critical of his behavior.

While I respected the lyrical dexterity and clear talent that X had, the XXXSENSATION project created in protest of his ascension, not despite but because of his violent persona. The project worked as a satirical examination of his bad-boy character through the recomposition of his works, removing the negativity and darkness and replacing them with positivity and brightness. It was clear XXXTENTACION was rapidly ascending the public consciousness by the people speaking out about his music (namely Kanye and Kendrick), but my project was more about the exposing and playing with the ‚Äúkayfabe‚Äù of rap; the artistic choices that rappers make to define and promote a persona.

The context of the culture at this particular time is important to understanding the nature of this project. Trump had just been inaugurated as the 45th president of the United States of America. It felt like America itself had become a parody, or a dismal episode of a Reality TV show. Rap music was in a strange place, with the racial unease and general darkness looming a lot of heads turned to rappers preaching positivity as a different approach to the violent, gang-affiliated trap music that was coming out of Atlanta (e.g., Future, Migos, Young Thug).

XXXSENSATION recognized this yearning for something uplifting, but it was important that it still sounded dope. There were rappers who were trying to be that positive voice, but they ended up sounding unintentionally corny (looking at you Kyle & Lil Yachty). The only rapper who seemed to do this authentically was Lil B, but the world wasn't ready for that yet. Instead, the zeitgeist was focused on trap music‚Äîspecifically triplet flows about whipping up dope coming out of Atlanta.

XXXTENTACION (XT) came into the game with #BADVIBESFOREVER as his mantra. His whole aesthetic was negativity, violence, and misogny. As a counterpoint, XXXSENSATION's (XS) position was #GOODVIBESFOREVER and his songs, tweets, and merch reflected this positivity and contrast.


As XT's star grew, he began to shift his energy away from negativity and, in doing so, started copying the very actions that XS had done months prior. Most notably, in May 2017 XS began a "hashtag challenge"‚Äî#OneMillionForOurWomen‚Äîto raise $1,000,000 for women's rights and victims of domestic abuse.
